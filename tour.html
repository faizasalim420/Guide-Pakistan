Sindh

Mazar-e-Quaid:The Tomb of the Father of Pakistani Nation.

Mazar-e-Quaid, also known as the Mausoleum of the Founder, is the final resting place of Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan. Located in Karachi, Pakistan, Mazar-e-Quaid is a prominent national monument and a symbol of the country's independence and unity.

Thar Desert:also known as the Great Indian Desert.

The Thar Desert found in Sindh province, is not just some tourist spot or landscape but a living testament to the survival through different desertification events of more than a thousand years. Its history dates back to the Indus Valley Civilization era, one of the oldest Bronze Age urban cultures. The remains of ancient civilizations that resided in the Thar Desert show traces of advancements in terms of urban planning, engineering, and lifestyles.

Mohenjo Daro:also renowned as “the mound of the dead”.

Mohenjo Daro’s history is as old as 4,600 years ago. It was built around 2600 BCE on the Indus River’s west side with around 35,000 to 50,000 people living in it. It is considered to be one of the oldest cities in the world and the earliest from the Indus Valley Civilization’s culture of the Bronze Age. However, people left this city in 1900 BCE and the logic behind it is vague.
